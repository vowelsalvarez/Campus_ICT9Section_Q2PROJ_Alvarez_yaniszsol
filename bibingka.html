bibingka

Ingredients:

ingredients:

2 cups rice flour

1 cup coconut milk (can be fresh or canned)

1/2 cup sugar

1/4 cup butter, melted (you can also use margarine)

2 large eggs

1/4 cup sweetened condensed milk (optional, for extra sweetness)

1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder

1/4 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, for flavor)

Procedure:

- Procedure:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). If you have banana leaves, line the baking pan with them (grease the leaves with butter first to prevent sticking). Prepare the Bibingka Batter:

In a large mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Mix well. Add the melted butter, eggs, coconut milk, sweetened condensed milk (if using), and vanilla extract (if using). Stir the mixture until smooth and well combined. Prepare the Pan:

If you're using banana leaves, place them inside a 9-inch round cake pan or a similar-sized baking dish. Grease the leaves and the sides of the pan with butter or cooking spray to ensure the bibingka doesn't stick. If you're not using banana leaves, just grease the pan with butter or cooking spray. Pour the Batter into the Pan:

Pour the bibingka batter into the prepared pan, filling it about halfway or slightly more. Add Toppings:

if you're using salted eggs, arrange the sliced salted eggs on top of the batter. Sprinkle the grated cheese over the top as well. Optionally, you can also add some grated coconut on top before baking. Bake the Bibingka:

Bake in the preheated oven for 30 to 40 minutes or until the bibingka is golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Finish and Serve:

Once the bibingka is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve the bibingka warm, optionally with a dusting of grated coconut or a drizzle of sugar on top.
